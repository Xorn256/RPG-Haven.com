Fallout2, the sequel to the best RPG of 1997 is here. 80 years after the fall of the Master, the original hero's descendant must once again save his village from a horrible fate. The goal of this game is to find the legendary G.E.C.K. (Garden of Eden Creation Kit), and help others along your way.

No real changes in graphics or engine have been made, although the battle bar has had a make-over and the game itself looks a bit more luscious than the first. The biggest improvement above the first is the number of quests and valueable NPCs which may join you on your quest (if you're nice enough). Fallout2 uses a lot of the same graphics from the first, but that can be justified due to the fact that most original weapons are still in use (gotta love them uzis).














Although combat and journey interface are almost exactly the same as the first, it still seems fresh in comparison to some of the 360 degree role playing (it can get dull pretty quick). The amount of work spend on artistry and music is amazing to the extent of wondering how they fit everything on one CD. The starting movie and intro is a beautiful artistic touch, and the constant use of movies integrated with gameplay is great. Although, if your computer is not the best, you'll have some problems...

The all new music added has given Fallout a nice frightning and realistic soundtrack. Although the plot is different and graphics and music have been made from scrap--this really feels just like a Fallout add-on pack! Not to say the game is not great (it's wonderful), but a re-vamp in the engine could have been appreciated.

Since the setting is staged in Washington down, instead of just Nevada and California, the climate and creatures of the land have changed--for the worse (or better, if your thinking challenge wise). All new creatures such as the Wanamingos and Geckos abound. The ferociousness of these creatures are unmatched, even to the Death-claws, another creature making it's reappearance from the game.

Fallout 2 certainly ups the stage of post-nuclear role playing, but there is a lot more to be done... check for Fallout 3 sometime soon in our Previews section...
