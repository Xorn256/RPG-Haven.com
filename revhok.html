Review:Hokuten
By Jared Demers

Reviewed by the Boney Dragon


{Note within one .zip file there are four more .zip files. The fourth one is self-extracting but it did not work.}

Graphics: 4/10
Although there are a few nice areas, the graphics here suck, children. The overworld map looks bad and the dungeons look even worse. The author must've spent very little time on the towns because they (mostly) look bad too, especially the insides of buildings which are a few tiles surrounded with blackness. The tilesets used are the ones that came with the program. The graphics are unoriginal and disappointing.

Sound: 0/10
I give the sound a zero because there isn't any. There is not one note of music in the whole game. The only sound effects are the ones in battle which suck to begin with. Do not release a game when there is no sound!
Story: 6/10
The storyline is present but there are many problems. One thing is the fact that things seemingly happen for no reason. Why do I have to overthrow some king and only need to kill two people in doing so? The characters here suck as well, but there is some substance behind them. One good thing is the flashback, that was cool. The dialogue was both hilarious and horrible, depending on the part of the game. Overall, the story could be great if some polishing is done.

Playability: 5/10
Except one, all the game's teleporters worked right. However, many events were screwy and switches were used poorly. Why did the bridge suddenly become repaired? I didn't do anything special to it. The battles were OK but they need a lot of work. There is no variety of enemies and the battles are nothing but "choose fight a few times, use a potion, choose fight a few times, use a potion..." Give the characters some magic spells! Battles were balanced, but they were a real chore to play.

Overall Assessment
This game needs a lot of work if it is to become worthwhile. The graphics, sound and gameplay are all disappointing. Also, the "Hokuten Knights" were ripped off of Final Fantasy Tactics. I would also suggest compressing the game in a better way.
Grading Scale
A= Excellent game that stands out and is very professionally done. Great job!
B= Very good game that is well worth checking out, but could be improved.
C= There are a few things that are great, but the RPG is pretty average.
D= Mediocre at best. Big problems make it a chore to play.
F= Some have it, some don't. Some can't even pretend they have it. Crap alert!

Hokuten by Jared Demers: D-

-Boney Dragon
