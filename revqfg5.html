After years in the making, Quest for Glory 5: Dragon Fire finally broke into store shelves and dazzled hopeful QFG fans everywhere. Since the conception of the series, QFG has offered a more puzzling world, where most battling is optional or solvable otherwise. This may not appeal to a few, but Quest is a true RPG where your actions determine the outcome.

After surviving through four other games, our hero (you can load a character created from the first QFG to the second and so forth, thus keeping with your old items and stats) finds himself on a mission to find who assassinated the king, only to find others in high power all falling to a sinister plot. The beautiful 3d rendered character and hand-painted backgrounds add a nice touch to the game, and fully orchestrated music plays throughout the game.










The humor of the series still abounds in Dragon Fire, freshening up the game from what would be a fairly serious plot. Some jokes are right out, like the comments on items if you continually click on them and others a more subtle--only caught by an expert QFG player.

Plot is what makes the Quest for Glory series, and the fifth is no exception. An awesome plot which will take your character through the heavens and hell await a curious player. Multiple endings make playing smart worth while, and although you can beat the game through force, it's sometimes best to think your way through things.

The only one downside of the game is that Sierra's rush to get QFG out for Christmas made some promises impossible (like multiplayer!). A few big bugs are to be fixed with patches, but the multiplayer end of things is yeat to be seen. Although this is just a small scratch of a great game, it needs to be mentioned, since QFG is not PERFECT. No future games have been planned, but don't be suprised to see a Quest for Glory 6 in shelves some times in the 2000's.
