Review:Ancient Evil
By Ankylo

Reviewed by the Boney Dragon



Graphics: 6.5/10
The graphical element in this RPG is a tale of two cities. On one hand, it possesses the coolest graphic trick I have seen in an RPG Maker 95 game: moving water! Not only that, there is a nice title screen and a few custom tiles. I was also impressed with the earthquake. On the other hand, the graphics seem pretty rushed. Floors of buildings are nothing but the transparent hue, there was no border around water or snow or lava..., and things seemed to be in weird places. I am also getting sick of seeing the same tilesets. Sound: 6/10
This game demonstrates great use of sound effects. When doors opened and switches clicked, I could hear it. There were even more dynamic sound effects when stuff exploded or something. There was plenty of music in the game, but it was all ripped off from commercial RPGs. I am growing less and less tolerant of rip off music; it seems to be in every game.
Story: 6.5/10
Ancient Evil has a good story, but it needs some polishing. I liked the opening cinema and the story had a flow to it. However, the plot was very linear and there was no reason for stuff happening. Why do I have to go here? Why did this happen? I asked these questions often. A good way to bring all the quests and tasks together would be to have cinemas from the point of view of the enemies. Regarding the enemies, more character development is needed to make me actually care about them. This story had some good ideas but is confusing and vague.

Playability: 7/10
First off, there is better way to make a character go through a doorway than using a transporter. Secondly, I noticed that sometimes music would play at the wrong time or not at all. Thirdly, the people in towns were hard to talk to because they were zipping around all over the place. Either stop them or make them talk on contact. Now that the gripes are out of the way, I thought the battles were well done because they were challenging but not impossible. Teleporters worked most of the time, but the game's end was a little wierd. I fought an impossible enemy and died.

Overall Assessment
This game is very average. While it does have some good ideas and dialogue, nothing makes it stand out (other than the cool water) and it gets old really fast.
Grading Scale
A= Excellent game that stands out and is very professionally done. Great job!
B= Very good game that is well worth checking out, but could be improved.
C= There are a few things that are great, but the RPG is pretty average.
D= Mediocre at best. Big problems make it a chore to play.
F= Some have it, some don't. Some can't even pretend they have it. Crap alert!

Ancient Evil by Ankylo: C-

-Boney Dragon
