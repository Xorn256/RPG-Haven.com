One of the most famous games of all time has been recently released to the PC and has sold over 6 million copies in all! Although this review is kind of redundant, considering about everyone knows about Final Fantasy VII, but it must be made never the less.

Involving corporate sabotage and magic on the highest level, FF7 revolutionized the industry and made Squaresoft all the more popular. Only three other Final Fantasies made it to the shores of the USA, but another is on the way.

Cloud Strife is featured as the main hero along with many other characters that can join you along the way. The superb graphics involved with FF7 transfer even better to the PC (Final Fantasy's engine was so powerful the Playstation could'nt fully texture the polygons). Over four CDs the epic saga of corruption and questing unfolds with a great storyline and soundtrack.















The amount of monsters and variations on gameplay make this game enjoyable for months, and the secrets packed in to the game make it a joy to play over and over.

The London Symphony Orchestra composed much of the FF7 soundtrack, including many which sound better in original format (A One Winged Angel is far superior as an MP3 that the actual track).

The beautiful graphics utilizes top-of-the-line picture quality, and intergrates movies into the actual gameplay with such quality that you can not tell unless you try to.

This is one the of better games of all time!
