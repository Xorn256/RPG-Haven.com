Review:Rising Sun
By Graeme Langmaid

Reviewed by: Paladin



Graphics: 5/10
This game had pretty impressing graphics. Not many glitches (actually I found only two), and the author took his time to make this game glitch free. The tilesets used are not the original ones so they give the game a nice touch. But the interior of the houses is horrible. I am not lying when I write that there was no interior. The inside of a building was, floor and black stuff around it, very bad. The only furniture I saw was the inn table and the desk and bed in Swift's hut. This is not so good. Actually I can't say much more because the game is pretty short. But I loved the hunting range. Really nice and original.

Sound: 7/10
Actually the sound here is not good at all. The only music I heard was in the intro when you heard the FF3 battle music. The sound effects are a little better. There are a lot of them, (arrows flying, evil laugh and other stuff). I personally think that music is a really important part of a game though and this game has almost nothing in the way of music. sorry no high point here.
Story: 7/10
Well this game had a story, kind of. The game begins with four knight fighting a group of soldiers and killing them, after that they get killed too. Then you find out that an evil person took amulets that the knights had and used them to take over the world... bla bla. Since this is a beta you can't catch on to the story much. I hope this gets improved in a later release. The characters are somewhat original. One is a village boy and an other one is a hunter. They meet and follow each other because the hero Rand has to hunt a deer and needed the hunter's help. The info about the characters could be a little better but it can go as it is.

Playability: 5/10
There was actually a big bug in the game. You can't encounter any enemies. You get a error window and you continue playing. This should be fixed immediately. I saw no save points whatsoever in the game and in the party there were two women you couldn't talk to.

Overall Assessment
The game is somewhat good and is *maybe* a good choice for downloading. Well if you are an RPG maker, then you can download it to get inspiration. But it is hard to judge a game that is as short as this one. If the author got a little more engaged in making the game maybe it would be better.
Grading Scale
A= Excellent game that stands out and is very professionally done. Great job!
B= Very good game that is well worth checking out, but could be improved.
C= There are a few things that are great, but the RPG is pretty average.
D= Mediocre at best. Big problems make it a chore to play.
F= Some have it, some don't. Some can't even pretend they have it. Crap alert!

Rising Sun by Graeme Langmaid: C

-Paladin
