EverQuest A new and upcoming game currently in development by 989 Studios and published by Sony. This game in my opinion may be the first successful 3D pen and paper style, RPG game ever. It combines the good old mud engine that we've all come to love, with a very impressive 3D engine. The game's ability to support many people over the internet and still keep with a good role-play may in fact be its key to lay waste to older Internet role players such as UO, Gemstone, ect. Currently the game is in beta test. And will be expected to be released sometime 1rst quarter '99. As of now the engine looks as if it needs some work.
















But game isn't about graphics, however. It's about role-playing. Most role players I've met don't care about the graphics as longs as the game play is good. Which is what I believe many RPGers seem to have forgotten. But this game seems to have the perfect blend of both worlds. You start the game by creating the character you want to play. There are many different races to choose from including Gnomes, Trolls, Humans, Several different kinds of Elves, Barbarians, Erudites (a race of intellectuals), Dwarves and Ogres. Once you pick your race, you can customize the textures (picking from a large store of options), as well as what sex your character is. Then you go on to modify your statistics (Strength, Intelligence etc...) and you pick a class.

There are quite a few character classes to choose from, including the old standbys like fighters and clerics, as well as some unique classes like the Shaman or, a monk. Each has its own fighting techniques as well as unique homelands. At this point you're ready to jump into the game so you hit "enter world" and your quest begins...

The character customization is just a slight hint of the thought that went into the design of this game. Once you enter the world of Norrath, you will be stunned by how impressive it truly is. Since you start off in a town, you will initially wander around there. You will find NPCs to talk to, of course, but the real interaction comes from all of the player characters that are online with you. After you learn your way around the city of your choice (depending on race and class) you can venture out into the rest of the world. Norrath consist of three continents and 10 or so major cities. In the space of the game world you will find more variety than has been seen in any game. This is a complete world, and after looking around a bit you will come to believe it.

As much as the game looks to be promising, there are a few dark spots on its horizon. Recently the beta has been opened up to far more players and we're starting to see a much broader audience. Many of these new players don't care to stay in character and will speak using words like "dood" and "l8". It has been proven before that a lack of "In Character play" can ruin the game play of true RPGers. And a much higher percentage of players that seem uninterested in the experience of RPGing and want to figure out ways to annoy other people. Hopefully the PK ban that is currently in affect will keep them away in the long term.

The future addition of guilds will help some players with their obvious bloodlust towards others because guilds will be able to declare war on other guilds, thus allowing PK'ing between the two. This isn't added yet however, so only time will tell as to this games pathetic failure or great triumph. Because I believe that if the producers go the right way with the game it can be the greatest RPG ever, but if they don't... Well we all know the popularity of all those other 3D pen and paper based rpgs out there.
