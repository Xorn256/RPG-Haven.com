Septerra Core by Topware Interactive is looking good. FOcusing on a world composed of seven different layers, the game brings characters together through extreme circumstance and adventure. Each of the seven layers is meant to house a different life-style of people or creatures, meaning a trip to another layer can be vastly different from the other.

There isn't much for graphics available at this time, but a lot of the plot and character design has been released by Topware, and what seems to be coming along looks great. The beautiful graphics will use 16-bit 3d rendered sprites and rendered backdrops.




















All the characters, Araym, Badu, Corgan, Doskias, Grubb, Led, Lobo, Maya, Runner, and Selina will have involving backgrounds and skills. Some mechanics, some magic and others, each can be used to solve the games multiple puzzles in various fashions. There will be many puzzles, I have learned, since the actual world is one big one itself, and the center for the game.

The original creator of Septerra intended it to be a vast puzzle, only to be solved from the core. The core of Septerra is a moon-sized computer used to monitor and serve the entire planet. Over hundreds of generations, deposits of carbon were super heated to become diamond. Exactly once every hundred years, each layer of Septerra slides into an exact location, and a beam of light will strike to diamond, opening the only entrance into the core, provided you have both keys made by the creator.

Septerra Core takes a lot of engine elements from Squaresofts masterpieces like Final Fantasy 6 and Chrono Trigger. A lot of the traditional console gaming points will be introduced and used, to simplify and enhance gameplay. In fact, Septerra Core is strongly influenced by the Square element, and this makes for better gaming usually.

If all goes well, Septerra Core will be released semi-soon and will great strength. Assuming there are no bad mistakes made, Septerra Core will be one of the better RPGs of 1999/2000.
